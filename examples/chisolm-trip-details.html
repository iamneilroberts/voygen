üì± MOBILE PHONE & ESSENTIAL APPS

Phone Service Options for Ireland & UK

Option 1: AT&T International Day Pass

  • Cost: $12/day per line (only charged on days you use it)
  • What you get: Your regular plan's talk, text, and data
  • How to activate: Text "TRAVEL" to 4004 or call AT&T before departure
  • Pros: Keep your number, no setup hassles
  • Cons: Most expensive option at $120 for 10 days

Option 2: Physical SIM Card (Buy at Airport)

  • Where: Shops in Dublin Airport arrivals hall or Heathrow
  • Providers: Three, Vodafone, or O2
  • Cost: ¬£20-30 for 10-20GB valid 30 days
  • Setup: Staff will help install and activate
  • Important: Only works if your iPhone is unlocked (check with AT&T first)
  • Cons: You'll have a UK/Irish phone number

Option 3: eSIM (Can be tricky with AT&T phones)

  • Warning: Many AT&T iPhones have compatibility issues with European eSIMs even if "unlocked"
  • If you want to try: Airalo or Holafly apps
  • Cost: $15-30 for 5-10GB
  • Must check: Settings ‚Üí General ‚Üí About ‚Üí Carrier Lock (must say "No SIM restrictions")
  • Common issue: AT&T's network settings often block foreign eSIMs
Recommendation: Given the AT&T eSIM issues, either use AT&T International Day Pass for convenience (though expensive) or buy a physical SIM at Dublin Airport. The airport shops are used to helping American tourists and will test it before you leave the store.
WiFi Alternative: Both hotels have free WiFi. Most restaurants, cafes, and even the London Underground stations have free WiFi. You can use WhatsApp or FaceTime for calls over WiFi.
